About

CS background. Social science lens. Justice orientation.

My training is in computer science, but the questions that drive me are social and cognitive. I am more interested in what AI systems do to people than in building systems for their own sake.

That instinct has organised my research across public perception, disinformation, governance, language fairness, and what I call the invisible failure modes — the harms structurally excluded from our measurement frameworks before we even begin to audit.

I am currently a fellow at the BASE Fellowship (Black in AI Safety and Ethics), where my research investigates language-specific gaps in AI safety training data, with a focus on low-resource and African languages. I hold a B.Sc. in Computer Science from the University of Nigeria, Nsukka.

The question that has led everything I've done is basically: what happens to communities that are left out of the room when AI systems are designed? And more specifically — what does it cost them?
